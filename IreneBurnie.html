What is CMT?

Charcot-Marie-Tooth (CMT) is the most common inherited neurological disorder, affecting about 1 in 2,500 Australians.

CMT affects approximately 1 in 2,500 Australians

It damages peripheral nerves, leading to muscle weakness and sensory loss. Students with CMT often have the same intellectual potential as their peers but face physical barriers.

Important: CMT affects everyone differently—from mild symptoms to significant mobility challenges.

School Impacts

School demands like moving between classes, carrying books, and navigating crowded areas can cause excessive fatigue and pain for students with CMT.

Support Strategies

Physical Access

  • Wheelchair accessibility
  • Ground-floor classrooms
  • Elevator access

Reduce Fatigue

  • Early class dismissal to avoid crowds
  • Extra set of textbooks
  • Nearby consecutive classes

Adapt Tasks

  • Typing instead of handwriting
  • Oral presentations
  • Modified practical activities
